The Sound of Languages - An Aspiring
Polyglot's Love Story
By Natty2612

"What do you love doing the most?", "What are your hobbies?", "How do you spend your free time?". We've run into the variations of the aforementioned questions ever since school days, but at some point we've decorated our answers for what the situation asks for. We've all been there right? Whether it's to impress our crush about how endearing it might be if we told them about our interests to match theirs or to impress the interviewer to get the job, the purpose is IMPRESSS them. I guess atleast my 10 year old self was definitely devoid of that purpose because back then I loved watching TV and I would shamelessly mention it as my most loved activity. Well I know what you're thinking, "That's not a hobby, that's a passtime activity everyone does". Yes yes I get it, but hear me out first. You see, when you say you love watching TV, most of them would take it at face value but let me explain what I mean. When I watch TV, I consume information just like when I'm reading a book, I don't just watch content for fun -well that's actually a bonus- but I watch TV, movies, series and all kinds of entertainment media to learn how each language is spoken, how to sound like a native in a language, new words and phrases, funny expressions, witty comebacks and what not (Might appear as a justification to why I binge-watch series and movies, which umm I'm not denying ðŸ˜¬). Little did I know that underneath all of that hunger and curiosity to learn about a new language that I was actually getting better at it. I had a flair for learning languages. Now don't let that deceive you into thinking I am fluent in French or German or whatever the most popular language everyone's learning. No, the point I'm trying to get across is that I love learning a language I fall in love at first sound.
Being an Indian, South Indian particularly-you're inherently a multilingual. Why you ask? Because we have 23 official languages in India including English. I mean get this, I am originally from Kerala(a.k.a God's Own Country), my homestate which speaks Malayalam, my parents moved to the buzzling city, Bangalore which is the capital of Karnataka whose state language is Kannada. Schools here in Karnataka teaches Kannada, English and Hindi as mandatory subjects as part of our curriculum. Then we have this ginormous movie industry-Our Indian Cinema-churning out 1500 movies per year which makes us wanna be fluent in atleast one other language for entertainment's sake. There you go! We already know 5 languages by fate if you may say so. So factually speaking we're already polyglots. All I'm trying to say is, we've got bit of a headstart if we were to officially call ourselves Polyglots.
One of my proudest moment that I owe to my love for languages happened back in school during my 7th grade. I scored the highest in Kannada beating all of my fellow classmates, most of whom were native speakers. So our school Principal, having noticed that I managed to score the highest even though it's not my mother tongue, decided to praise me in front of the whole class. That right there was one of my happiest days. I owe my mother all of the credits for having discovered this trait in me. Although my mother tongue is Malayalam, growing up in a city of people that migrated from different states here looking for jobs in their early days, I only knew how to speak in my mother tongue, not read or write. That was up until I grew curious of what keeps my Mom engrossed in her Vanitha magazine, a monthly publication in Malayalam, which I believe was the only source that made my Mom feel at home while away from home. She saw me skimming through her beloved magazines only to find out there were two pages dedicated for kids to solve a word puzzle and a coloring activity, everything else was Latin and Greek to me at first glance. But something kept me picking up those magazines and wanting to know how to read. So my Mom finally made it her mission to get me to read Malayalam. You see, it's not really an essential skill, nobody expects you to know how to read in your native tongue, just knowing how to speak is an impressive thing when you live in a city like Bangalore.
And then the Hallyu (Korean) wave happened which took the world/internet by storm. So obviously, I moved on to my next love, Korean pop music, drama series and the language. It's the striking resemblance of the intonation, rhythm and tone Korean language holds with most of our Indian languages, that pulled me in and has anchored me for life. Half way into it now, and I've discovered that even the grammar structure is similar. And you see, Youtube's algorithm of recommendations sucked me into the world of Thai BL series, followed by Filipino movies and Spanish music. Right now there's an Indonesian song "Dunia Tipu-Tipu" By Yura Yunita that's on repeat.
So to sum it up, if someone were to ask me those same questions today, I'd say "I love learning new languages and proudly call myself a Linguaphile". Wow!! I can't believe I rambled on about it just to answer one question. I don't know where this love of mine is gonna take me on forward in this journey, but I'm sure as heck loving it - learning about the language, its people and the culture, might as well come in handy when I'm out traveling the world while making new friendsðŸ˜‰.
